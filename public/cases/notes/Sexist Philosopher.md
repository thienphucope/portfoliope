**The Biological Mechanism Behind the Variance:** The reason for this "Greater Male Variability" lies in genetics. Since females have two X chromosomes, they benefit from a "backup" system; if one gene is faulty, the other can often compensate, leading to more stability and consistency in traits like intelligence. In contrast, males have an XY pairing. Because the Y chromosome is much smaller and lacks many of the genes found on the X, any mutation or extreme trait on their single X chromosome—whether exceptionally good or bad—is more likely to be expressed. From an evolutionary standpoint, this makes males the "experimental" sex, tending toward high-risk, high-reward biological strategies to secure better genes, even if it means a higher likelihood of negative outcomes.

There have always been, and will always be, women at the peak of intellectual achievement.
